## 2. K1 — the instrument is about *agency*, not primarily measurement

The agents converged hard on **measurement/honest-reading** as the instrument's defining job
(`03 §1 C1–C2`). The author's `OQ-1` answer relocates the centre of gravity:

> *"go away with the current state of software that tries to predict where the user is going…
> give back power to the user… the system reacts to the user and not the other way around."*

So the differentiator the agents were reaching for is not ultimately *"it surfaces a reading"* —
it is *"it does not act on, steer, or anticipate the user."* The honest readout is the **means**
(it puts the data in front of the user so the *user* interprets and decides), and "operated, not
used" (`P6`) is the **behavioural signature** of that means. The **end** is user agency:
the human remains the initiator of action and the sole interpreter of the data.

This is a cleaner resolution of the `T2` tension (`03 §2`) than the synthesis reached. The
conversation-as-product case looked like a hole only because Q1 was defined as "must surface a
single reading." Under the agency framing, a chat app is not a contradiction — it is fine *as
long as it is not engineered to predict, hook, or harvest.* The test a concept must pass is not
"can it be made into a readout?" but **"does it keep the user as the initiator and interpreter,
free of anticipatory manipulation?"**

*Balance / what is genuinely lost:* the agency framing is broader and softer than the
measurement framing, so it is also **less visually prescriptive**. "Surface an honest reading"
told you what to draw; "keep the user as initiator" does not. The measurement/readout vocabulary
remains the brand's most concrete and recognisable expression — it should be kept as the *default
strong form* for inquiry/monitoring products, with agency as the governing principle above it.
The two are not rivals: agency is the *why*, the instrument readout is the *most characteristic
how*.

## 3. K3 — the "presentation can show false data" weakness is a scope boundary, not a flaw

The synthesis named T3 — the look lends credibility to a system whose underlying data may be
false — as one of the three sharpest weaknesses (`03 §2 T3`). The author's `OQ-7` answer reframes
it precisely:

- The brand guarantees **presentational honesty**: it will not fabricate a meter, fake progress,
  manipulate, or stamp `0x00` on a tracking surface. It shows *the real data it was given.*
- It does **not** guarantee **substantive honesty** of arbitrary input — *"a user that inputs
  false data will see this data displayed… the app shows real data it got, but can't necessarily
  verify its trueness, nor is it its role most of the time."*
- This is true of **any** system under the same criterion; it is an **application-domain
  concern**, not a defect of the philosophy. The brand is *"an ideal to reach, guidelines to
  follow,"* not a validator of world data.

The correct analytic status of T3 is therefore **not "weakness" but "boundary of scope."** The
original criticism was valid only against a claim the brand never made (that the UI certifies the
*truth of the data*, vs. the *honesty of the presentation*). With the scope stated, the critique
neutralises. Two honest riders remain (these are *not* retracted):

1. For software whose **purpose** is verification (where confirming data trueness *is* the job),
   substantive honesty is back in scope and the brand's presentational guarantee is not enough on
   its own. The author acknowledges this class exists.
2. The author raises an **ecosystem-level lever**: the brand may *"explicitly and intentionally
   refuse to develop certain software"* that is contrary to its philosophy. This converts a
   per-product worry into a **portfolio/selection policy** — a new, deliberate mechanism not in
   the current documents (carried to §5 as a real open item, because the *criteria* are unstated).

## 4. K5 — the rejection rules are about *patterns*, not *forms*

`VISUAL_IDENTITY.md §4` lists SaaS-dashboard, chat-client, and feed as things a screen "must never
feel like," and `DESIGN_USE.md` G1 treats uniform metric grids as dashboards to avoid. Read
literally, these forbid whole UI forms and leave no successor — which is exactly what produced
OQ-3/6/8. The author's answers (`OQ-3/6/8`) supply the missing distinction:

- The brand opposes the **attention-economy behaviour** of these forms — infinite engagement,
  prediction, notification-baiting, manipulative defaults (consistent with `ETHOS C5.1`).
- It does **not** oppose the **form** where the task intrinsically requires it. A navigation app
  needs a map; a chat product needs a transcript; an ops console needs many live metrics. In
  those cases the work is **visual integration into the Lab language**, not usage transformation,
  and *"if it is too divergent, keeping a familiar UI/UX is the right move."*
- The governing aim across all of them is **"less attention-seeking usage, while remaining
  pragmatic."**

So the `§4` "must never feel like" list is better read as **"must never adopt the manipulative
mechanics of,"** not "must never take the shape of." The author explicitly flags that the document
phrasing invites the stronger misreading and *should change* — recorded here as an analysis
implication (not applied): the anti-target rules want a one-line qualifier separating *rejected
pattern* (engagement/anticipation/dishonesty) from *permissible intrinsic form* (the shape a task
genuinely needs), so a kept dashboard/chat/map is understood as conformant, not as a failure.

## 5. What remains genuinely open after these answers

The answers close most of the list; three items survive, now sharper and smaller:

- **U1 — Per-feature visual-integration research (was OQ-3/6).** For intrinsic forms (conversation,
  map, many-metric console), *how* they read in the Lab language is decided **case by case** and
  has not been done. This is acknowledged, scoped design work — not a contradiction. It needs a
  lightweight rule of thumb ("preserve the form; remove the attention-seeking mechanics; apply
  chassis/seam/`0x00` where they don't fight the task") rather than a per-form spec.
- **U2 — Ecosystem refusal criteria (new, from OQ-7).** The brand may decline to build software
  that is "enemy to its philosophy." The *mechanism* is stated; the **criteria** are not. What
  disqualifies a product (e.g. anticipatory-by-nature, engagement-monetised, verification-critical
  beyond presentational honesty)? This is a genuine, unwritten policy fork.
- **U3 — Honest *estimated* progress, concretely (was OQ-7-open).** The principle is settled —
  honest estimation is allowed, fabricated precision is not (`OQ-7`). What is still unwritten is
  the **presentational convention** that visibly distinguishes an *estimate* from a *measurement*
  (so an estimate doesn't read as a false-precision meter). A small, real design question.

## 6. Updated answers to Q1 / Q2 / Q3 (superseding `03 §4`)

**Q1 — What is an "instrument" in NC-750?**
An instrument is software that **keeps the human as the initiator of action and the sole
interpreter of the data** — it *reacts to* the operator rather than predicting, steering, or
acting on them (`OQ-1`; `VISUAL_IDENTITY.md §3 P6` "operated, not used"). Its most characteristic
behaviour is to put an **honest, live reading in front of the operator** so the operator (not the
system) draws the conclusion (`§2`, `DESIGN_USE.md §6, §10`) — but the readout is the *means* and
agency is the *end*. It is a behavioural/epistemic stance, not a look (the chassis can be present
while the stance is absent). Pragmatic exceptions are explicit: prediction-by-purpose software
(e.g. scientific tools) is fine **provided the user initiates it** (`OQ-1`).

**Q2 — How can current UI concepts be transformed toward this worldview?**
Subject each concept to one governing question: **does it keep the user as initiator and
interpreter, free of anticipatory or attention-harvesting manipulation, and does it present its
state honestly?** (`OQ-1`,`OQ-7`; `DESIGN_USE.md §10`; `ETHOS C5.1, C5.5`). Transform the concept's
*mechanics* toward that end; **preserve its form when the task intrinsically needs it** and
integrate it visually rather than forcing a metaphor (`OQ-3/6/8`). This is a **re-grounding of UI
in honesty and user-agency, expressed through a legible documentary grammar** — not a re-skin, not
minimalism, and **not a blanket rejection of dashboards/chats/feeds** (only of their manipulative
patterns). Honesty here is *presentational* — the UI won't fake or manipulate what it shows;
verifying the truth of underlying data is an app-domain concern, generally out of brand scope
(`OQ-7`).

**Q3 — What is the essence of the UX of NC-750 and its philosophy?**
**Give the user back authorship of their computing: the software reacts, presents its real state
honestly, and never predicts, steers, or harvests — and that honesty + that authorship are the
things of value the user owns.** Honesty is *presentational and governing* (the brand will lose a
sale before breaking it — `OQ-2`) and ownership is multi-layered (data: local-first/hard-delete
`C1.3/C1.5`; keys: BYOK `C3.1`; identity: the seed fingerprint `P7`). The brand **proposes** this
worldview, it does not impose it, and it deliberately self-selects like-minded users (`OQ-2`). Its
register is moving from absolutist to **pragmatic, realistic honesty** (`OQ-5`), and its visual
language is an evolving, **deliberately flexible** mimicry of physical instruments — dynamic,
modal-capable, status-reactive — not a fixed static panel (`OQ-9`).
